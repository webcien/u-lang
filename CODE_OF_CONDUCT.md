# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone who participates in the U community will be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of their background, identity, or beliefs.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing and accepting feedback gracefully
- **Collaboration**: Working together toward common goals
- **Empathy**: Understanding different perspectives and experiences
- **Professionalism**: Maintaining professional conduct in all interactions
- **Inclusivity**: Welcoming people of all backgrounds and experience levels
- **Accountability**: Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, discrimination, or intimidation
- **Hate Speech**: Slurs, derogatory comments, or hateful language
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or disruptive behavior
- **Spam**: Unsolicited commercial messages or off-topic content
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances or comments
- **Threats**: Any form of threats or intimidation
- **Abuse**: Personal attacks or abusive language

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Community events and meetings
- Official communications
- Any space where the community gathers

This includes behavior by project maintainers and contributors.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Email**: conduct@u-lang.dev (confidential)
2. **GitHub**: Contact a project maintainer directly
3. **Anonymous**: Use our anonymous reporting form (coming soon)

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident
- Who was involved
- When it occurred
- Any relevant links or evidence
- How it affected you

### Investigation

All reports will be:
- Taken seriously and investigated promptly
- Handled confidentially
- Investigated fairly and impartially
- Documented for transparency

### Response

Depending on the severity, responses may include:

1. **Warning**: For minor violations
2. **Temporary Ban**: For serious violations (24 hours to 30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban**: For severe or repeated violations
4. **Escalation**: To law enforcement if necessary

### Appeals

If you believe a decision was unfair, you may appeal by:
1. Sending a detailed explanation to conduct@u-lang.dev
2. Providing new evidence or context
3. Requesting a review by a different maintainer

---

## Responsibilities

### Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate action in response to violations
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions
- Banning users for violations
- Maintaining confidentiality of reporters

### Contributors

Contributors are responsible for:
- Following this Code of Conduct
- Reporting violations
- Respecting others' boundaries
- Assuming good intent while addressing concerns

---

## Conflict Resolution

If you have a conflict with another community member:

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve it directly and respectfully
2. **Mediation**: Ask a maintainer to help mediate
3. **Formal Process**: If unresolved, use the formal reporting process

---

## Diversity and Inclusion

We are committed to:

- **Welcoming all backgrounds**: Race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, nationality, etc.
- **Accessibility**: Making the community accessible to people with disabilities
- **Representation**: Actively working to increase diversity in leadership
- **Inclusive Language**: Using gender-neutral and inclusive language

## FAQ

### Q: What if I accidentally violate the Code of Conduct?

A: We understand that mistakes happen. If you realize you've violated the Code of Conduct, we encourage you to:
1. Acknowledge the mistake
2. Apologize sincerely
3. Explain what you'll do differently
4. Make amends if possible

Most violations are resolved through dialogue and education.

### Q: What if I'm not sure if something violates the Code of Conduct?

A: If you're unsure, it's better to ask! Contact a maintainer or email conduct@u-lang.dev with your question.

### Q: Is this Code of Conduct enforced?

A: Yes, absolutely. We take violations seriously and will enforce this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly.

### Q: Can I appeal a decision?

A: Yes, you can appeal any decision by providing new evidence or context. Appeals are reviewed by a different maintainer.

### Q: How do I report anonymously?

A: We're working on an anonymous reporting form. In the meantime, you can:
- Use a temporary email address
- Ask a trusted community member to report on your behalf
- Email conduct@u-lang.dev with "ANONYMOUS" in the subject line

### Q: What happens after I report?

A: We will:
1. Acknowledge receipt of your report
2. Investigate the incident
3. Determine appropriate action
4. Notify you of the outcome (without revealing details about the person reported)
